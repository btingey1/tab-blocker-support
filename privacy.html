Privacy Policy

Tab Blocker for Safari

Support Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 1, 2025

No Data Collection

Tab Blocker does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data or browsing information. Period.

What Tab Blocker Does

Tab Blocker is a Safari Web Extension that prevents websites from opening unwanted new tabs. It works entirely on your device.

Your list of enabled sites is stored locally in Safari's extension storage on your device. This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us or any third party.

No Analytics or Tracking

Tab Blocker does not include any analytics frameworks, tracking pixels, crash reporters, or third-party SDKs. There are no servers to send data to.

No Network Requests

Tab Blocker makes zero network requests. It operates entirely offline. No data is transmitted to any server, ever.

Your Browsing History

Tab Blocker does not access, read, or store your browsing history. The extension only checks whether a site is in your locally-stored enabled list to determine whether to block new tabs.

Children's Privacy

Tab Blocker does not collect any data from anyone, including children under 13. The app is safe for users of all ages.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated, changes will be posted on this page with a revised date. Tab Blocker's core principle will not change: we do not collect your data.
